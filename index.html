Design Rationale: This classroom layout supports Common Core writing standards, as suggested by Tompkins et al. (2021) on pages 347-348, through intentional spatial organization that facilitates student choice, which answers question 8, differentiated instruction, which answers question 6, and structured writing routines, which answers question 7. For example, the classroom has a variety of attributes that can serve as writing routine tools, and it can scaffold writing learning through the practice of writing fluency, as suggested by the Writing Center. It also supports other writing developmental areas, as suggested by the map. The map also supports a variety of learners through its resources, which allows for differentiated instruction. For example, it has an array of resources that can fit a diverse range of student needs, such as an English Learner or any struggling student needing a visual of a word from the word wall. The map also has an interactive whiteboard that can serve as hands-on instruction that could be more intriguing to ADHD students or any other gifted individuals.
